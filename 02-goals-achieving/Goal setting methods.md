# Goal setting methods
**The 1-3-5 Rule**: This method involves setting one big goal, three medium goals, and five small goals for a given period. This approach helps you prioritize your goals and focus on what’s most important.

**The Warren Buffett Strategy**: This method involves creating a list of 25 goals and then selecting the top five most important ones. The remaining 20 goals are then considered distractions and should be avoided until the top five are achieved.

The Eisenhower Matrix: This method involves categorizing your goals into four quadrants based on their urgency and importance. This approach helps you prioritize your goals and focus on what’s most important.

The SMARTER Method: This method involves setting goals that are specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, time-bound, evaluated, and readjusted. This approach helps you create goals that are well-defined and achievable.

The WOOP Method: This method involves setting goals that are Wishful, Outcome, Obstacle, and Plan. This approach helps you identify potential obstacles and develop a plan to overcome them.

The OKR Method: This method involves setting Objectives and Key Results for a given period. This approach helps you focus on what’s most important and measure your progress.
- **Objectives:** These are qualitative statements that describe what you want to achieve. They should be ambitious, inspiring, and easily understandable. [![OKR Objectives](https://encrypted-tbn3.gstatic.com/images?q=tbn:ANd9GcQl_Csf1NIe8rMmCX1RqYg8hfA2jA9acR1U3SWtb10XYJuBwGLllSIV66op5Vm1 "OKR Objectives")](https://weekdone.com/resources/objectives-key-results)
- **Key Results:** These are measurable outcomes that track progress toward achieving the objectives. They should be specific, time-bound, and ambitious (typically aiming for 70% completion)
- **How OKRs Work:**

1. **Set OKRs:** Begin by defining a few (typically 3-5) objectives that align with your overall strategy. Then, for each objective, identify 3-5 key results that will measure progress.
2. **Track Progress:** Regularly track and measure progress toward the key results. This allows you to identify roadblocks, make adjustments, and celebrate successes.
3. **Review and Reflect:** At the end of each cycle (typically quarterly or annually), review the OKRs and reflect on what was achieved and what could be improved for the next cycle.

4. **Regular Check-ins:** OKRs are typically set for a specific time frame, often on a quarterly basis. Regular check-ins, usually weekly or bi-weekly, help teams and individuals track their progress, discuss challenges, and make adjustments as needed.

5. **Scoring and Grading:** At the end of the set period, the achievement of Key Results is evaluated. Teams or individuals score each Key Result on a scale, often from 0 to 1.0, indicating the degree of completion. The scores are then aggregated to assess overall objective success.

6. **Learning and Iteration:** The OKR process encourages a culture of continuous improvement. Teams reflect on what worked well, what didn't, and what can be done differently in the next cycle. This learning and iteration process helps refine goals and strategies over time.

The Backward Planning Method: This method involves starting with your end goal and working backward to create a plan to achieve it. This approach helps you create a clear path to success.

The 80/20 Rule: This method involves focusing on the 20% of your goals that will yield 80% of the results. This approach helps you prioritize your goals and focus on what’s most important.

The Mind Map Method: This method involves creating a visual map of your goals and how they relate to each other. This approach helps you see the big picture and identify potential obstacles.

The Accountability Partner Method: This method involves finding an accountability partner who will help you stay on track and achieve your goals. This approach helps you stay motivated and accountable.

OGSM stands for Objectives, Goals, Strategies, and Measures. It is a strategic management framework that is commonly used in business and organizational planning. Each component of OGSM serves a specific purpose in guiding the development and execution of a business strategy:

1. **Objectives (O):** Objectives are high-level, overarching statements that define what an organization aims to achieve. They are typically qualitative and provide direction for the organization.

2. **Goals (G):** Goals are specific, measurable targets that support the achievement of objectives. Unlike objectives, goals are more concrete and can be quantified, allowing for easier tracking and assessment.

3. **Strategies (S):** Strategies are the broad approaches or plans that an organization will implement to reach its goals. These are the action plans or initiatives that outline how the goals will be achieved.

4. **Measures (M):** Measures, also known as Key Performance Indicators (KPIs), are the metrics used to assess the progress and success of the strategies. They provide a way to measure and evaluate the performance of the organization in relation to its goals.

The OGSM framework is often presented in a cascading manner, where objectives at the top level guide the creation of goals, which in turn inform the development of strategies, and finally, measures are identified to monitor and evaluate progress.

This strategic planning tool helps organizations align their activities with their overall vision and mission, providing a structured approach to achieving success and managing performance.
